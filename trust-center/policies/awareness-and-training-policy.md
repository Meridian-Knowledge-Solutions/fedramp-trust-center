# Security Awareness and Training Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements for Meridian Knowledge Solutions' security awareness and training program. The program ensures that all personnel understand their security responsibilities, organizational policies, and the proper use of information systems to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data within the FedRAMP environment.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all Meridian employees, contractors, and other personnel who have access to systems within the FedRAMP authorization boundary.

---

## 3. Policy Owner

The **Director of Federal Cybersecurity Operations** is responsible for the development, implementation, and maintenance of this policy and the associated training program.

---

## 4. Policy Requirements

### 4.1. General Security Awareness Training

* **Initial Training:** All new personnel must complete security awareness training as part of the onboarding process, *before* being granted access to production systems.
* **Annual Training:** All personnel are required to complete security awareness training at least annually.

### 4.2. Role-Based Training

* Personnel with specific, elevated security roles and responsibilities (e.g., system administrators, developers, incident response team members) must receive additional, role-based training specific to their duties.
* This training shall be provided before assuming their role and as needed when systems or responsibilities change.

### 4.3. Training Content

The general security awareness training program includes, at a minimum:
* User responsibilities in protecting company and customer data.
* Recognition and reporting of security incidents, including phishing and insider threats.
* Best practices for password management and multi-factor authentication (MFA).
* Acceptable use of Meridian systems.
* Physical security and clear desk/clear screen policies.

---

## 5. Training Delivery and Tracking

* **Delivery:** All training is assigned, delivered, and tracked using Meridian's internal Learning Management System (LMS).
* **Tracking:** The Director of Federal Cybersecurity Operations is responsible for monitoring training completion and ensuring compliance.
* **Records:** The LMS will serve as the system of record for all training activities, including completion dates and training content.

---

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

* **Director of Federal Cybersecurity Operations:** Manages and updates the training program and content; monitors and reports on compliance.
* **People Operations (HR):** Ensures new hires are enrolled in initial training during onboarding.
* **Managers:** Ensure their team members complete all required training in a timely manner.
* **All Personnel:** Responsible for completing all assigned training and adhering to the security principles learned.

---

## 7. Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed and approved at least **annually**, or upon significant changes to the environment or security requirements.

---

## 8. Enforcement

Failure to complete mandatory security awareness and training may result in suspension of system access until training is completed, and may lead to disciplinary action.
