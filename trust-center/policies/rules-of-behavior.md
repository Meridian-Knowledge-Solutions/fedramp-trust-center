# Meridian Knowledge Solutions - Rules of Behavior

## 1. Purpose

These Rules of Behavior (RoB) define the security responsibilities and acceptable conduct for all personnel accessing the Meridian Knowledge Solutions FedRAMP-authorized environment. Access to this system is a privilege that requires you to protect its resources and data.

## 2. Scope

These rules apply to all Meridian employees, contractors, and authorized partners who are granted access to any system or service within the FedRAMP authorization boundary.

## 3. Acknowledgment

You must read, understand, and formally acknowledge these Rules of Behavior **before** you are granted access. This acknowledgment must be renewed **annually**.

Failure to comply with these rules may result in disciplinary action, up to and including removal of access and termination of employment or contract.

---

## 4. General Responsibilities

As a user of this system, you **MUST**:

* **Protect Your Credentials:** You are responsible for all actions taken using your unique account. Never share your password, access keys, or multi-factor authentication (MFA) device.
* **Report Incidents Immediately:** Report any suspected security weakness, incident, or policy violation (like a lost laptop, a phishing attempt, or unauthorized access) to `security@meridianks.com`.
* **Use for Authorized Business Only:** Access the system and its data only for the explicit, official duties of your job role.
* **Adhere to Least Privilege:** Do not attempt to access data or functions for which you are not authorized.
* **Comply with US Persons Requirements:** All personnel with administrative access to the FedRAMP environment must be U.S. Persons. You must report any change in this status immediately.

## 5. Prohibited Activities

As a user of this system, you **MUST NOT**:

* **Bypass Security Controls:** Do not attempt to disable, bypass, or interfere with any system security measure (e.g., logging, monitoring, MFA).
* **Introduce Malicious Code:** Do not intentionally or recklessly introduce malware, viruses, or other malicious code.
* **Store or Process Unauthorized Data:** Do not upload, store, or process data that is not explicitly authorized for the system. This especially includes:
    * Classified government data.
    * Any data that is at a higher impact level than the system is authorized for (e.g., PHI, sensitive financial data).
* **Exfiltrate Data:** Do not copy, download, or move any data from the authorized environment to an unauthorized location (like a personal computer or public cloud drive) without explicit approval.
* **Use Unauthorized Software/Services:** Do not install or connect any unapproved software, applications, or third-party services to the environment.
* **Access Inappropriate Sites:** Do not access personal email, social media, or other non-business-related websites from within the secure environment.
* **Use Work Credentials Externally:** Do not use your Meridian FedRAMP credentials to register for any public or third-party websites.
